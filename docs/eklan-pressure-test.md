# Eklan Pressure Test

## Day 6 – Eklan Pressure Test

These scenarios are designed to simulate realistic ICU emergencies where you must respond immediately and professionally without prompts or guided phrases.

---

### Scenario 1: Sudden Desaturation

It is 2:15 AM in the ICU. Mr. Miller suddenly becomes restless in bed. The monitor alarm begins sounding loudly, and his oxygen saturation drops rapidly from 95% to 79%.

Mr. Miller grabs at his oxygen mask and says:

“I can’t breathe… help me…”

His breathing becomes fast and shallow, and thick mucus is visible in the suction tubing.

---

### Scenario 2: Aggressive Withdrawal Behavior

Mr. Miller wakes up confused and angry. He attempts to pull out his IV line and shouts loudly at the ICU staff.

“Leave me alone! I want to go home! I need a drink!”

His heart rate is elevated, and he appears sweaty and agitated.

Emily, his daughter, looks frightened beside the bed.

---

### Scenario 3: CRRT Machine Alarm

Mrs. Thompson’s CRRT machine suddenly starts beeping continuously during dialysis.

The machine displays a pressure warning message, and the blood flow slows down.

Mrs. Thompson looks anxious and asks:

“Is something wrong with the machine? Am I okay?”

---

### Scenario 4: Post-Ventilator Panic

A few minutes after ventilator removal, Mr. Miller suddenly begins coughing repeatedly and becomes extremely anxious.

“Why can’t I breathe normally?!”

He tries to sit upright quickly and reaches toward his chest while breathing heavily. The respiratory therapist has not arrived yet.

---

### Scenario 5: Family Member Emotional Breakdown

After the emergency event, Emily begins crying near the nurses’ station.

“I thought my father was dying…”

She asks repeated questions while ICU staff continue treating patients nearby.

At the same time, another monitor alarm sounds in a nearby room.

---

### Scenario 6: Refusal of Treatment

Mr. Miller refuses breathing exercises and pushes away the incentive spirometer.

“I’m too tired. I don’t want to do this anymore.”

He continues coughing weakly and refuses to make eye contact.

His lung sounds remain congested.

---

### Scenario 7: Medication Confusion

Mr. Miller looks suspicious while Nurse Sujin prepares medication.

“What are you giving me now? Is this going to knock me out again?”

He hesitates before taking the medication and asks multiple questions quickly.

---

### Scenario 8: Emergency Call from Another Room

While checking Mrs. Thompson’s dialysis treatment, an emergency alarm sounds from another ICU room.

A staff member rushes in and says:

“Room 8 needs immediate assistance! The patient is rapidly desaturating!”

Nurse Sujin must respond immediately while managing both patients safely.

---

### Scenario 9: Emotional Support Dog Incident

Charlie, the emotional support dog, suddenly begins barking loudly during a stressful moment in the ICU. Mr. Miller becomes more anxious as staff move quickly around the room.

Another patient nearby appears disturbed by the noise.

Emily tries unsuccessfully to calm the dog down.

---

### Scenario 10: End-of-Shift Emergency

Near the end of Nurse Sujin’s shift, Mr. Miller suddenly develops severe coughing and shortness of breath again.

The monitor shows declining oxygen saturation, and he appears exhausted.

Dr. Harris asks Nurse Sujin for a rapid update on the patient’s condition while the respiratory therapist prepares emergency equipment.

Several ICU staff members are now waiting for information from Nurse Sujin.
